## What to Draw

Three bryophyte specimens arranged side by side in equal-width panels across the image. Each specimen is shown in profile/side view, growing on a small patch of soil or rock at the bottom. The three panels are separated by subtle vertical gaps (no lines or borders). Leave ample space above each specimen for overlay markers.

### Panel 1 (Left Third): Moss

**Position**: Centered in the left third of the image (x: 10-33%)
**Visual**: A cluster of small bright green moss plants (cushion growth form), approximately 3 cm tall. Each plant has a distinct upright stem with tiny overlapping leaves. Two or three plants have tall thin stalks (seta) rising above the leafy portion, each topped with a small brown capsule (sporophyte). At the base, show fine thread-like rhizoids (not roots) extending into the substrate. The moss sits on a small mound of dark brown soil on a gray rock surface. Color: rich emerald green leaves (#2E7D32), brown capsules (#5D4037), brown soil (#3E2723), gray rock (#9E9E9E).

### Panel 2 (Center Third): Liverwort

**Position**: Centered in the middle third of the image (x: 37-63%)
**Visual**: A flat, ribbon-like thallus (body) growing horizontally along the substrate surface. The thallus is dark green with a slight sheen, showing a branching, lobed pattern that resembles a liver shape. The surface has a subtle patterned texture (air pores visible as tiny dots). A few small umbrella-shaped structures (gemmae cups) sit on the upper surface. At the base, show fine rhizoids extending downward. The liverwort sits on moist dark soil. Color: deep forest green (#1B5E20), slightly glossy surface, dark brown soil (#3E2723).

### Panel 3 (Right Third): Hornwort

**Position**: Centered in the right third of the image (x: 67-90%)
**Visual**: A rosette of flat, dark green lobed thallus at the base, with several tall, thin horn-shaped structures (sporophytes) rising vertically from the center. The horns are 4-6 cm tall, slender, dark green at the base fading to brown at the tip, and slightly curved. The thallus is more circular and compact than the liverwort. At the base, show fine rhizoids. The hornwort sits on moist dark soil. Color: dark green thallus (#2E7D32), brown-tipped horns (#4E342E), dark brown soil (#3E2723).

## Layout Notes

- Each specimen should occupy roughly 30% of the image width with 3-5% gaps between panels
- Specimens should be vertically centered with the base (soil line) at approximately 70% from the top
- Leave at least 15% clear space above the tallest structures for overlay markers
- Leave at least 10% clear space below the soil line
- All three specimens should be at the same scale relative to each other so size comparisons are meaningful
- The soil patches should appear as natural, slightly irregular mounds — not geometric rectangles
