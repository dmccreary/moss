## What to Draw

A cross-section view of a single moss leaf cell (parenchyma cell), magnified to fill the frame. The cell should be roughly rectangular with slightly rounded corners, oriented horizontally. The viewing angle is a cross-sectional slice as if viewed under a light microscope. Leave a 5% margin on all sides for overlay markers.

### 1. Cell Wall

**Position**: Outermost boundary of the cell, forming the entire perimeter
**Visual**: A thick tan/beige (#D2B48C) border, approximately 3-4% of the cell width, with a slightly fibrous cellulose texture. Rounded rectangular shape.

### 2. Cell Membrane

**Position**: Immediately inside the cell wall, running parallel to it
**Visual**: A thin golden-yellow (#DAA520) line, about 1% width, pressed against the inner surface of the cell wall. Slightly wavy to suggest fluidity.

### 3. Central Vacuole

**Position**: Center of the cell, occupying approximately 55-60% of the interior area
**Visual**: A large, pale blue (#B0D4F1) region with a slightly translucent quality and a thin membrane border. Irregularly rounded shape suggesting fluid pressure.

### 4. Nucleus

**Position**: Off-center, positioned at approximately 30% from the left edge and 35% from the top, pressed between the vacuole and cell wall
**Visual**: A dark purple (#6A0DAD) roughly circular structure, about 12% of the cell width. Contains a smaller, darker nucleolus (#3D0066) visible inside. Surrounded by a double-membrane nuclear envelope.

### 5. Chloroplasts

**Position**: Distributed around the periphery of the cell, between the vacuole and cell membrane — approximately 8-10 oval bodies
**Visual**: Bright green (#228B22) lens-shaped ovals, each about 5-6% of cell width. Internal structure shows faint stacked thylakoid grana as darker green lines within each chloroplast. Arranged in a ring around the cell edges.

### 6. Cytoplasm

**Position**: Fills all space between the vacuole, organelles, and cell membrane
**Visual**: Pale yellow-green (#F0F5E0) with a slightly granular texture suggesting cytosol with ribosomes. Semi-transparent quality.

### 7. Endoplasmic Reticulum

**Position**: Near the nucleus, extending from the nuclear envelope toward the cell periphery, approximately 35% from left and 55% from top
**Visual**: A network of folded membranes in light purple-pink (#D8BFD8), with some portions studded with tiny dark dots (ribosomes on rough ER). Appears as undulating parallel lines.
