#### 2. In the "Rehydration Lab," students place dried moss on a petri dish and add measured amounts of water. What scientific skills does this lab primarily teach?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. Gene sequencing and DNA extraction
2. Measurement, data recording, and the concept of water retention
3. Chemical synthesis and molecular modeling
4. Astronomical observation and star mapping
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **B**. The Rehydration Lab teaches fundamental scientific skills: measurement (mass before and after rehydration), data recording (documenting changes over time), and the concept of water retention (calculating the water-absorption ratio). These skills are accessible to students at multiple grade levels and produce results within a single class period, making it an efficient and engaging hands-on activity.

#### 7. What is citizen science, and how does it apply to moss study?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>

3. Scientific research conducted by non-professional volunteers who collect data, often using platforms like iNaturalist
4. A type of moss that only grows in urban areas
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **C**. #### 9. When designing a moss garden for accessibility (wheelchair users, people with limited mobility), what is the most important design consideration?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. Using only preserved moss that requires no interaction
2. Raising planting surfaces to accessible heights and ensuring smooth, wide pathways for wheelchair access
3. Eliminating all moss and replacing it with artificial turf
4. Requiring all visitors to wear specialized equipment
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **B**. Accessibility design for moss gardens includes raising planting surfaces to heights reachable from a seated position (approximately 60-80 cm), providing smooth, wide pathways (at least 120 cm) for wheelchair access, using firm ground surfaces, and ensuring sensory elements like misting stations are within reach. The goal is to make the therapeutic and educational benefits of moss accessible to people of all abilities.

    **Concept Tested:** Accessibility Design

#### 10. A high school teacher wants students to design a controlled experiment using moss. Which of the following correctly identifies independent and dependent variables?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. Independent: moss growth rate; Dependent: light intensity
2. Independent: amount of water applied; Dependent: number of species in the forest
3. Independent: light level (distance from window); Dependent: moss growth after three weeks
4. Independent: student's grade; Dependent: temperature of the classroom
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **C**. In a properly designed experiment, the independent variable is what the researcher intentionally changes (light level, controlled by placing moss at different distances from a window), and the dependent variable is what is measured as a response (moss growth after three weeks). This "Light Gradient Lab" connects to photosynthesis concepts and introduces the idea of limiting factors through genuine experimental design.

    **Concept Tested:** Simple Experiments
